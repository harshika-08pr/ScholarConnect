Documents for scholarship applications

Use this page as a quick checklist before applying. It explains which documents are usually needed, who issues them and how to upload them correctly so your form is not rejected for simple mistakes.

Key documents at a glance

Document Who issues it? Why is it needed?
Income certificate Tehsil office / municipal office To prove family income for need-based scholarships.
Caste certificate State / district authority To claim reserved-category benefits (SC / ST / OBC etc.).
Bonafide / enrollment certificate Your school / college To confirm that you are currently studying in that institute.
Previous year marksheet School / board / university For merit-based and renewal scholarships.
ID proof / Aadhaar Government of India For identity verification and linking payments.
Bank passbook / cancelled cheque Your bank branch To receive scholarship amount directly in your account.

Quick checklist before you apply

  • Check the official scholarship notice to confirm the exact list of required documents.
  • Make sure your name, date of birth and parents’ names are written the same everywhere.
  • Keep both original documents and clear scanned copies with you while filling forms.
  • Note down issue dates and validity (some income / caste certificates have limited validity).

Need help with documents?

If you are not sure which proofs are needed for a particular scholarship, first:

  • Open that scholarship on ScholarConnect.
  • Read the “Eligibility” and “Required documents” sections slowly.
  • Note down anything you do not understand.

After that, you can contact your school/college office or use the Support section on ScholarConnect to clarify your doubts.

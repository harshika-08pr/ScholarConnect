PM-USP: Central Sector Scheme of Scholarship (CSSS)

Merit-cum-means support for Class-12 toppers now in college — central scheme via NSP.

CSSS provides financial help to bright students from lower-income families to continue UG/PG studies. Typical benefits and renewal rules are published by the Ministry/NSP; amounts commonly used in recent years are ₹12,000 p.a. for UG (first 3 years) and ₹20,000 p.a. for PG / later years of 5-year courses. Confirm amounts and rules in the scheme notice on NSP / Ministry pages. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

  • Level: UG / PG regular degrees (not distance) — scheme varies by course-type.
  • Typical eligibility: Good Class-12 performance (board percentile cutoffs), family income limit (example: ≤ ₹4.5 lakh — check current year), admission to a recognised college.

National Scholarship Portal (NSP) — detailed guide

NSP is the Government of India’s central portal for many scholarships. You must register once (One-Time Registration — OTR) and use that OTR to apply for schemes each year. Always open the scheme’s PDF (Guidelines) on NSP before applying. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}

Step-by-step: How to apply (generic NSP flow)
  1. Step 1 — OTR (One-Time Registration): Go to scholarships.gov.in → New Registration / OTR. You will need Aadhaar/EID, mobile number and email. (OTR gives you a 14-digit OTR ID.)
  2. Step 2 — Complete profile: Login with OTR ID & password, fill in personal, address, institute, and bank details carefully — these are reused across applications.
  3. Step 3 — Choose scheme: Under “Apply for Scholarship”, select the academic year and the scheme you want (the list shows only schemes open for your profile).
  4. Step 4 — Fill scheme form: Provide scheme-specific answers, upload scanned documents (PDF/JPG) as per the scheme PDF guidelines (size & format matter).
  5. Step 5 — Institute verification: Submit the form — your institute verifies details, then state / nodal officer verifies and the sanction is processed.
  6. Step 6 — Track status: Use NSP dashboard to track institute verification, state verification and payment status (PFMS/DBT details appear after sanction).
Documents commonly required on NSP
  • Aadhaar or alternate identity (as allowed by the scheme).
  • Passport-size photo (JPEG) and scanned signature if required.
  • Bank passbook front page / cancelled cheque (student name, account number, IFSC).
  • Income certificate (if scheme has income eligibility) — issued by the competent authority.
  • Caste / category certificate (SC/ST/OBC) if applicable, PwD certificate for disability benefits.
  • Last passed examination marksheet and current year admission proof / fee receipt.
Tips & common pitfalls
  • Check the scheme PDF for exact income cutoffs and supporting document formats before uploading.
  • Ensure Aadhaar is linked to the bank account if DBT (direct benefit transfer) is required by the scheme.
  • Institute verification is mandatory — follow up with your college/institute after submission.

PM-USP: Central Sector Scheme of Scholarship (CSSS) — full details

The Central Sector Scheme supports meritorious students from lower income backgrounds to continue higher education. Typical benefit patterns and renewal rules are published in the official circular and on NSP — examples of commonly reported amounts: ₹12,000 p.a. for undergraduate years and ₹20,000 p.a. for postgraduate / later years of integrated 5-year courses. Always confirm the current academic year brochure on NSP / Ministry sites. :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}

Eligibility (summary)
  • Passed Class-12 and enrolled in regular UG/PG degree (not distance) at a recognised institute.
  • Board percentile / cutoffs — scheme sets minimum percentile in Class-12 (check current year PDF).
  • Family income must be below the scheme limit (example: ≤ ₹4.5 lakh — verify current year).
  • Not receiving duplicative central/state scholarship where rules prohibit double benefits.
Documents checklist
  • Class-12 marksheet / percentile proof.
  • Admission/fee receipt for current academic year.
  • Income certificate (format as per state rules / scheme PDF).
  • Bank passbook (Aadhaar-linked account recommended), Aadhaar or alternate ID.
  • Caste / disability certificate where applicable.
How to apply (point by point)
  1. Register / login on NSP (One-Time Registration).
  2. Complete profile and choose “PM-USP Central Sector Scheme of Scholarship” (scheme names may vary slightly on NSP).
  3. Fill all fields and upload documents exactly as the PDF specifies (rename files if needed, check sizes).
  4. Submit, then ensure institute verifies. Track status on NSP dashboard until payment (DBT) appears.
Renewal & continuation
  • Renewal usually requires minimum percentage/attendance criteria — check the scheme PDF.
  • Keep bank and Aadhaar details unchanged during the period of sanction for smoother DBT transfers.

AICTE Pragati Scholarship — detailed

AICTE Pragati is directed at female students in technical diploma/degree courses approved by AICTE. Recent notices indicate significant per-student amounts (commonly reported around ₹50,000 per year) and family income caps — always confirm exact numbers & cutoffs in the current AICTE / NSP notification. :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}

Who is eligible (summary)
  • Female students admitted to 1st year of an AICTE-approved degree/diploma OR 2nd year lateral entry as specified.
  • Family income usually restricted (example thresholds like ₹8 lakh appear in some notices — check current year).
  • Maximum number of girls per family may be limited (e.g., two in some notifications).
Documents you will typically need
  • Admission letter / seat allotment / fee receipt showing tuition charged.
  • Class X/XII mark sheets (proof of qualification), ID proof (Aadhaar).
  • Income proof (income certificate / salary slips / ITR) of parents/guardian.
  • Bank account details (passbook/cancelled cheque) in student’s name.
How to apply (point by point)
  1. Open scholarships.gov.in / NSP when AICTE Pragati is accepting applications.
  2. Login using your OTR ID (One-Time Registration) or create OTR first.
  3. Find the Pragati scheme in the list, carefully read the scheme PDF and FAQs.
  4. Fill the form, upload scanned documents as requested (pay special attention to income evidence and the fee receipt), submit and follow up with your institute for verification.
Tips
  • Keep a clear copy of admission / fee receipt showing tuition (this is commonly required for validation).
  • If your family has variable income (seasonal or agriculture), check which proof is acceptable (income certificate / ITR).

INSPIRE — Scholarship for Higher Education (SHE)

INSPIRE-SHE is administered by DST and targets students who choose natural & basic sciences at UG/PG level and meet high merit criteria (for example, often top 1% in Class-12 or specified rank thresholds). The INSPIRE portal provides the official application form, endorsement formats and FAQs. :contentReference[oaicite:13]{index=13}

Typical eligibility routes
  • Students within top 1% of the Class-12 board in eligible subjects, pursuing B.Sc / Int. M.Sc / B.S etc.
  • Or rank-based eligibility via national tests / KVPY etc. (see INSPIRE FAQ for exact accepted ranks).
Documents checklist
  • Class-12 marksheet / rank proof (board certificate / rank card).
  • Admission proof to eligible basic science course / institute.
  • Endorsement form from college principal (INSPIRE format — available on their portal).
  • ID proof (Aadhaar), bank details, passport photo.
How to apply
  1. Register on the INSPIRE online portal (online-inspire.gov.in).
  2. Complete the application, upload documents (endorsement form by college may be required), submit and track application status.
  3. INSPIRE has specific formats for some documents (endorsement, performance reports) — download these from the portal before applying.

PMSS — Prime Minister's Scholarship Scheme (Kendriya Sainik Board / DESW)

PMSS is aimed at widows and wards of ex-servicemen / ex-Coast Guard personnel for professional education. Exact eligibility, stipend levels and application procedures are published on DESW / Kendriya Sainik Board websites and vary by year. Always follow the official brochure. :contentReference[oaicite:14]{index=14}

Eligibility & documents (typical)
  • Proof of parent/guardian’s service/discharge (service record, PPO, discharge book).
  • Relationship certificate to the ex-serviceman / ex-Coast Guard (as applicable).
  • Admission proof in eligible professional course (fee receipt, admission letter).
  • Bank account (Aadhaar-linked where required), ID proof, photos.
How to apply
  1. Visit the official DESW / KSB PMSS page (links change; the authoritative pages list the current online portal and brochure).
  2. Read instructions and brochure carefully for required documents and deadlines.
  3. Register / apply via the specified online portal, upload documents and retain acknowledgement for tracking.
Tip
  • Contact your local Zila Sainik Board / Sainik Welfare Department for help with service documents if you have difficulty obtaining originals.
